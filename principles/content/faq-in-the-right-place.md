# FAQs where they belong

> **Category:** Content · **medium-low impact**
> A FAQ answers common questions without making the visitor leave the page. What an effective FAQ looks like, how to check yours, and when it's actually needed.
>
> 🇧🇷 [Versão em português](../../principios/conteudo/faq-presente.md)

A FAQ earns its place when it answers the real questions that stall a decision. A visitor on the verge of converting clears a doubt in a well-placed FAQ and goes on to do what you want. A FAQ full of vague answers or questions nobody's asking? It takes up space and changes nothing. Having a FAQ doesn't matter; what matters is whether the answers clear the blocker.

## Why it matters

- Visitors with a specific question reach the bottom of the page and find the answer nowhere. The intent to convert is there, but the unresolved doubt kills the decision. A well-placed FAQ sits between the pitch and the CTA, answering exactly what the visitor is thinking in that moment.
- The payoff of a FAQ isn't cosmetic, it's cutting last-mile friction. A visitor who's read everything, bought the pitch, but still wonders about delivery time, payment options or the cancellation policy won't go hunting for that answer elsewhere; if it's not there, they leave without converting.
- A FAQ also exposes what you failed to communicate on the rest of the page. When the same question keeps coming up, it's a sign your offer, your audience or your business model didn't land, and the FAQ is patching a bigger hole. The ideal FAQ answers legitimate questions the page genuinely can't avoid, not the ones that should have been answered earlier.

## How to check your page

- [ ] Are the questions phrased in your visitor's real language, or in company jargon?
- [ ] Does each answer resolve the doubt in 2-3 lines, or does the visitor give up reading?
- [ ] Do the questions cover the real blockers (timeline, price, compatibility, guarantee), or are they generic, like "What do you do?"?
- [ ] Is the FAQ somewhere visible (usually above the fold or right after the pitch), not buried in the footer?
- [ ] If an undecided visitor scans for words like "expensive", "timeline", "cancel" or "refund", does the FAQ answer them, or only cover the basics?

## Common mistakes

- **Questions nobody asks** — A FAQ stuffed with answers to nothing: "Who are you?" when the About section sits right above it. A visitor who got this far already knows; they want timeline, price or guarantee.
- **Answers too generic to help** — Question: "What's the timeline?" Answer: "Depends on the project." The undecided visitor reading that is still undecided. A real answer: "5-7 days for standard; 2-4 weeks for custom."
- **FAQ as a band-aid for weak copy** — When the value prop is fuzzy, the price is hidden or the audience is unclear, the FAQ becomes cosmetic tape. The visitor stays confused; a FAQ can't recover clarity missing from the main sections.
- **Clunky or overloaded accordion** — A section that doesn't invite reading, answers in heavy video, tiny type. The visitor closes it before reading. If the question matters, the answer has to be fast to take in.

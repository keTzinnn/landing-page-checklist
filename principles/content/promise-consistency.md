# Promise held from headline to button

> **Category:** Content · **medium-low impact**
> Check that the headline's promise is backed up by the copy, design and CTA, with no disconnect that pushes visitors away.
>
> 🇧🇷 [Versão em português](../../principios/conteudo/consistencia-da-promessa.md)

An effective landing page opens with a clear promise in the headline. But a promise you don't keep is a bait-and-switch. If the body copy, the benefit sections, the images and the call to action don't reinforce exactly what the headline pledged, the visitor feels the mismatch and leaves.

## Why it matters

- The headline promises a specific transformation, benefit or solution. When the visitor scrolls down and hits vague, generic content, or copy that drifts off the original point, trust breaks. Research on web reading patterns shows the first impression sets an expectation; any contradiction forces a re-read or a bounce, and both drive your rejection rate up.
- That inconsistency also wrecks perceived coherence: Cialdini's consistency principle shows people trust messages that stay aligned from start to finish. If the final CTA asks for a different action than the opening promise implied, the visitor starts doubting what the page is really after, and doesn't convert.
- High-performing landing pages run a straight line from promise to evidence to action. The body copy amplifies the headline, it never contradicts it. The visual design reinforces the message. The CTA closes the loop right where the headline pointed.

## How to check your page

- [ ] Does the headline announce a specific benefit, result or solution?
- [ ] Does every body section reinforce or expand on that promise without introducing new, off-message ideas?
- [ ] Do the images and visuals illustrate the promise rather than distract with side topics?
- [ ] Does the CTA ask for the action the headline reader expects, with no detours or surprises?
- [ ] Does the copy avoid vague, generic jargon that waters down the opening promise?

## Common mistakes

- **Headline sells one benefit, copy turns into a feature manual** — Headline: "Take control of your finances in 5 minutes." The copy then walks through 47 technical features. The visitor came for simplicity, not complexity.
- **Overblown promise the CTA can't deliver** — Headline: "Earn $10k/month, guaranteed." CTA: "Buy the $297 course." The page promises a result but only offers access to content, with no guarantee. The visitor smells a bait-and-switch.
- **Too many promises at once** — The headline tries to sell savings, speed, security and exclusivity all at the same time. None of them lands. The visitor can't tell which problem the page actually solves.

# Text that's comfortable to read

> **Category:** Content · **medium-low impact**
> Poor font size, line length and contrast hurt comprehension. We audit your typographic structure against established reading patterns.
>
> 🇧🇷 [Versão em português](../../principios/conteudo/legibilidade.md)

Unreadable text is invisible. When the font size, line length or contrast fail, the visitor simply can't read your value proposition. The landing page becomes visual noise instead of communication.

## Why it matters

- Per Nielsen's principle, people don't read the web line by line, they scan. Type that's too small forces re-reading and abandonment. Lines that run too long scatter the eye and cut comprehension, making the page tiring to read.
- Low contrast demands extra visual effort. Accessibility research (WCAG, the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) sets minimum contrast thresholds to keep text legible for readers with typical vision and with mild visual impairments alike.
- Line length is tied to reading flow: the sweet spot of 50-75 characters matches how the eye naturally moves. Outside that range you get fragmentation (lines too short) or drift (lines too long, losing your place on the next one).

## How to check your page

- [ ] Is the body font large enough to read comfortably on screen (not cramped)?
- [ ] Does text-to-background contrast meet the accessible standard (WCAG AA: 4.5:1 ratio)?
- [ ] Does line length (the width of the text block) stay between 50 and 75 characters, avoiding fragmentation or drift?
- [ ] Does the type hierarchy (font sizes and weights) visually separate the headline, body and call to action?
- [ ] Does line spacing (line-height) give the text room to breathe (typically 1.5 or more for body copy)?

## Common mistakes

- **Not enough contrast between text and background** — Light gray text on a slightly lighter gray background, below the accessible threshold. The visitor has to strain to read. The result: slower reading, faster exit.
- **Long lines with no break** — A paragraph spans the full width of the first screen (1200px+). The eye loses its place on the return to the next line. Comprehension drops.
- **Body font too small** — Body copy at 11px with no breathing room between lines. The visitor zooms in reflexively, breaking the natural flow. Conversion drops.
- **Inconsistent font weight** — Body at weight 300 (thin), headings at weight 700 (bold). With no clear visual contrast between, the reader can't parse the content hierarchy.

## Sources

- Nielsen Norman Group: "Legibility, Readability, and Comprehension" — Jakob Nielsen and researchers on font size, line-height and column width in web contexts.
- WCAG 2.1 (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines): Success Criterion 1.4.3 Contrast (Minimum) — the international spec for a minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio.
- Steve Krug: "Don't Make Me Think" (revisited edition) — principles of reading on the web and how layout shapes comprehension.
- CXL Institute: Typography and Design Best Practices — applying legibility in practice on landing pages and for conversion.

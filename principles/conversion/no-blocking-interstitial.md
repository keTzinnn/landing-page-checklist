# Arrival with no pop-up covering the content

> **Category:** Conversion · **high impact**
> Why banners, modals, and pop-ups that cover content or the CTA on arrival lower conversion. How to detect and fix them, and what the audit measures.
>
> 🇧🇷 [Versão em português](../../principios/conversao/sem-interstitial-bloqueante.md)

The visitor lands on your page and sees, first thing, what you want them to see — or an obstacle? Pop-ups, banners, and modals that cover content or action buttons on arrival turn the page into a maze. The result is abandonment before they've even read. Compliance (a discreet cookie strip) is fine; covering the CTA and content is conversion predation.

## Why it matters

- The recency effect makes the first impression carry outsized weight in the decision: a page that opens with an obstacle starts with friction already cashed in. The visitor has hundreds of alternatives — the odds they fight a modal to read your pitch are slim.
- Google penalizes intrusive interstitials that cover relevant content on arrival, particularly on mobile, hurting organic search visibility. The algorithm treats a blocking overlay as a signal of disrespect for the user experience — because it is.
- Landing-page usability research documents that overlays on initial load significantly reduce form completion and conversions: the visitor leaves before engaging. Newsletter or promo banners injected mid-read are worse still, because interest has already built and the physical interruption destroys the momentum.

## How to check your page

- [ ] Does no overlay (modal, banner, pop-up) cover any part of the primary CTA when the visitor arrives (first 3 seconds)?
- [ ] Is the above-the-fold content accessible without closing or scrolling past a dialog box?
- [ ] Do cookie or compliance banners take up at most a thin strip of height (< 10% of the first screen) and dismiss easily?
- [ ] Do discount, newsletter, or upsell pop-ups appear only AFTER the visitor engages (scroll, time, intent signal) — not on arrival?
- [ ] On mobile, does no overlay resize the first screen or force a zoom-out (tested on a real device, not just the browser)?

## Common mistakes

- **Blocking newsletter or promo modal on arrival** — The visitor lands and sees a big box asking for an email before they even know they want anything. Result: most abandon just to close it, and many leave the site without returning.
- **Cookie banner covering the action button** — The 'accept cookies' strip is the size of a real banner and blocks the primary CTA. Legal compliance becomes a conversion barrier.
- **Pop-up 2 seconds after arrival** — The visitor has read nothing, doesn't yet know they belong here, and the page throws an obstacle at them. The read is 'this page has something to hide' — because it's hiding the content.
- **Cascading overlays** — Closed the newsletter modal? Here comes the discount one. Then the feedback one. Each layer of friction doubles the exit rate and erodes conversion progressively.

# Converting without forcing account creation

> **Category:** Conversion · **high impact**
> Whether the page lets visitors convert without creating an account first. When account creation comes before the trial, it suppresses pre-conversion engagement.
>
> 🇧🇷 [Versão em português](../../principios/conversao/sem-conta-forcada.md)

Visitors who have to create an account before acting often abandon the page. The reason isn't a weak offer — it's that the friction of an account arrives BEFORE the benefit is ever experienced. This principle checks one thing: can the visitor convert (submit a form, start a trial, buy) without being forced to create a login?

## Why it matters

- A visitor at checkout who must register before paying faces double friction: trust (is it safe to hand over my data?) and effort (will I remember this password?). E-commerce behavior research shows that forcing account creation is a barrier on par with a long form — both are pre-conversion friction.
- In lead-gen and acquisition SaaS, the effect is amplified: the visitor hasn't scrolled the page, hasn't seen the cases, hasn't tested the value. Asking for an account here is like demanding ID at the door of a showroom. The capture form already collects contact info; forcing an extra set of credentials is redundancy that trips the least-effort principle (Fogg: motivation × ability × prompt; low ability = abandonment).
- When the account comes LATER (trial-then-login, free version with no login), the friction flips: the visitor has proof of value, has built initial trust, has invested time. Now the account isn't friction — it's protection for their own data. Product-led growth confirms that users onboard with more confidence after they've tried.

## How to check your page

- [ ] Does the page allow form submission/conversion without requiring account creation at the same time?
- [ ] If a trial/demo exists, can the visitor access it without a login required first (login comes later)?
- [ ] Does the capture form ask ONLY for contact info (email/phone), or does it also demand a username/password as part of the submit?
- [ ] If the page is e-commerce/transactional, is guest checkout possible, or does it force registration before payment?
- [ ] Is there microcopy or a modal explaining why an account will be created — is consent clearly communicated, or does the account appear as a surprise?

## Common mistakes

- **Account fields baked into the form** — A contact/demo form asks for email, phone, AND username/password in the same submit. The visitor has to choose a username before testing anything. The friction is concentrated right at the decision point.
- **Login required before the trial is reachable** — A trial or free version exists, but the visitor must create an account BEFORE entering. There's no pre-login preview. Without prior experience, the motivation to finish registration drops sharply.
- **Forced account at checkout** — E-commerce or SaaS redirects to a sign-up form at the cart, with no 'guest' or 'express checkout' option. For comparison, Stripe, Shopify, and Figma offer guest checkout — they lower friction BEFORE asking for trust.
- **Surprise password after the form** — The visitor fills out a form, hits submit, and gets an email: 'your account has been created.' There was no explicit consent — the conversion turned into forced onboarding.

# Action button visible without scrolling

> **Category:** Conversion · **high impact**
> A CTA reachable without scrolling on arrival. Position is objective; effectiveness is contextual and depends on the page and the audience.
>
> 🇧🇷 [Versão em português](../../principios/conversao/cta-acima-da-dobra.md)

The first screen (above the fold) is where the visitor decides to stay or leave. An action button in that zone lets them convert immediately, with no scroll friction. Position is measurable; the impact on conversion rate varies with the page, the audience, and the type of offer.

## Why it matters

- It removes friction on the shortest path to action. If the visitor has to scroll to find how to act, the odds of abandonment rise — especially on mobile, where the fold is smaller and more things interrupt (notifications, distractions). Immediate access to the CTA signals confidence and a clear sense of purpose.
- Objectively detecting position (above/below the fold) lets you measure it consistently; relative effectiveness depends on context (a one-click conversion offer works above the fold; a long pitch may need scroll plus a repeated CTA). The goal here is simply to make sure the option exists at the top.

## How to check your page

- [ ] Is there a primary action button visible on the first screen, without scrolling (checked against a standard first-screen height)?
- [ ] Is the button reachable on mobile (not desktop-only; tested on a typical phone screen)?
- [ ] If the page is long, is there an additional CTA repeated below, keeping the action accessible throughout the scroll?
- [ ] Does the above-the-fold action button match the headline's promise (without veering off to a secondary offer)?
- [ ] Does no overlay, modal, or cookie banner cover the CTA or make it unreachable on load?

## Common mistakes

- **No CTA above the fold** — The only button sits below the scroll (in a 'Get started' or 'Pricing' section further down). The visitor scrolled, didn't find it, and left — common on institutional landings that prioritize education over action.
- **CTA hidden by an overlay on load** — A modal form, cookie notice, or promo pop-up covers the primary button on arrival, forcing the visitor to close or accept before engaging with the real page.
- **Secondary CTA up top, primary one below** — The top offers 'Learn more' or 'Contact,' while 'Buy now' or 'Sign up' only appears after a scroll. A visitor who wants to act fast never found the path.

# Button that reappears down the page

> **Category:** Conversion · **high impact**
> Long page, CTA only at the bottom? The visitor scrolls, reads, and now has to scroll back up. Repeating the CTA strategically removes that conversion friction.
>
> 🇧🇷 [Versão em português](../../principios/conversao/cta-repetido-em-pagina-longa.md)

Long pages are the norm for landing pages: a hero with the offer, a benefits section, case studies, objections handled. But if the action button only reappears at the very end, a visitor who has scrolled the whole page now has to choose: scroll back up to click, or close the tab. Repeating the CTA strategically along the scroll removes that friction — and conversion is all about removing friction.

## Why it matters

- On a long page the visitor consumes content progressively. Each section shifts where the CTA sits on the first screen — and scroll behavior differs between mobile and desktop. On mobile, someone who scrolled three sections may already be convinced, but instead of clicking they have to scroll back or hunt for the button, which costs attention and drops conversion.
- Hick's Law says more options slow the decision down. The reverse applies here: the absence of a visible option (a CTA off-screen) forces a side action — scrolling to find it. Every extra click or step between conviction and conversion lowers the rate, because the intent to act is volatile and distractions are competing for attention.
- Long landing pages are long precisely because each section answers an objection: 'why this product?', 'does it actually work?', 'what does it cost?'. The visitor reads until convinced — and needs to act at that moment, not after scrolling a whole page back up.

## How to check your page

- [ ] Does the primary CTA appear in the hero?
- [ ] After each logical section (benefits, case studies, objection handling), does the CTA reappear — or only at the very bottom of the page?
- [ ] Is it the same button (same text, same color) in every position, or does it change and confuse?
- [ ] On mobile, does the CTA get covered by the keyboard or by floating elements within thumb reach?
- [ ] Does the repetition avoid turning into clutter? (Example: 10+ CTAs on a 5-section page = noise.)
- [ ] If the page has multiple steps (choose a plan, add an email), does each step have its own visible CTA?

## Common mistakes

- **CTA only at the end** — Hero + benefits + cases + FAQ + CTA. A visitor convinced halfway scrolls down, ends up at the bottom — and only there sees the button. Often they've already lost the impulse and have to scroll back to find it.
- **Floating CTA that vanishes on scroll** — A button fixed to the bottom (mobile) that disappears the moment the visitor starts scrolling. Convenient for hiding an ugly page, bad for conversion.
- **Different CTAs in each section** — 'Learn more' in the hero, 'Get started' in section 2, 'Free trial' in section 3. The inconsistency creates confusion — the visitor can't tell whether each button does something different or it's just sloppy writing.
- **CTA repeated too often (clutter)** — A button in every paragraph. The repetition stops being convenient and becomes spam — it kills the hierarchy and trains the visitor to ignore the button (banner blindness).

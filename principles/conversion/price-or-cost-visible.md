# Price or cost findable on the page

> **Category:** Conversion · **high impact**
> The visitor finds the price or an explicit cost reference without leaving the page, or follows a clear path to it.
>
> 🇧🇷 [Versão em português](../../principios/conversao/preco-ou-custo-visivel.md)

When a visitor lands on a page, one of their first questions is "how much does it cost?". Keeping the answer far away — or worse, hidden behind a sign-up — creates friction. The principle isn't "price must be above the fold," but rather: there's a clear signal, on the page or immediately reachable, that answers or points to the answer.

## Why it matters

- Hick's Law suggests every extra decision (hunt for the price, go through steps) costs cognition. Consumer-psychology research (Cialdini's principle of transparency) shows that uncertainty about cost is high-magnitude friction — it drives abandonment before the visitor even considers the product. When the price or a cost anchor is visible, the brain classifies fast: 'is this in my budget zone?' If yes, they engage; if not, they leave cleanly. Both beat a silent abandonment.
- Transparency about price lowers decision friction before commitment. For SaaS, that includes signals like 'start free,' '30-day free trial,' 'no card required' — zero-cost entry anchors that show confidence and lower the barrier to try. Platforms that expose a price range or billing model see more consistent engagement: the visitor quickly judges whether the cost is in their zone instead of bouncing on uncertainty.
- Price opacity is a deliberate tactic in specific contexts (custom B2B, premium retail), but on a public conversion landing page — especially SaaS — it breaks the cognitive contract with the visitor: 'I came here to decide fast, not to reverse-engineer my budget.' Cost transparency signals product maturity and commercial confidence.

## How to check your page

- [ ] Does a price, price range, or billing model appear above the fold or in an explicitly named section ('Pricing,' 'Plans')?
- [ ] If there's no fixed price, is there a clear zero-cost-entry signal ('Start free,' '14-day free trial,' 'No card,' or equivalent) visible on the page?
- [ ] Is there a navigation path from one action (click 'Pricing' or a short scroll) to the answer, with no paywalls or login required before revealing it?
- [ ] If the page details a specific plan, is there a clear link to compare with other plans or return to the full pricing lineup?
- [ ] Does secondary copy (CTAs, FAQ) explicitly address or defuse cost concerns ('No surprises,' 'No long-term contract,' 'Included')?
- [ ] Is the price range kept current, or is there an explicit disclaimer noting it needs to be confirmed with sales?

## Common mistakes

- **'Contact for pricing' as the default** — The CTA leads to a contact form or chat before revealing any range. The visitor gives up because basic information already demands a conversation. Fix: show an estimated range (e.g. 'From $500/mo') and offer contact for custom adjustments.
- **Price buried away from main navigation** — 'Pricing' hidden in the footer or only reachable after scrolling 80% of the page. A visitor in a hurry never gets there. Fix: a sticky menu, an above-the-fold section, or a prominent pricing item in the nav.
- **Multiple currencies or models without context** — The page shows USD and another currency, monthly and annual, without making clear which is the default or recommended. It paralyzes the choice. Fix: detect location or offer tabs with a single toggle (currency/cycle).
- **'Talk to sales' with no starting range** — Copy like 'Custom pricing — talk to our team' with no order-of-magnitude anchor. High-touch B2B can work this way, but a public landing loses early engagement. Fix: 'Starting at $X. Custom plans — book a demo.'

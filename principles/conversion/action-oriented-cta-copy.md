# Button copy that sells the action

> **Category:** Conversion · **high impact**
> Button copy names the specific benefit or action and echoes the headline, instead of generic words like Submit or Sign up.
>
> 🇧🇷 [Versão em português](../../principios/conversao/copy-cta-orientada-acao.md)

The button label is the last thing a visitor reads before converting. When CTA copy spells out the concrete benefit or the specific action, it closes the loop on the headline's promise and removes friction — the visitor knows exactly what happens on click. "Submit" or "Sign up" leave that open.

## Why it matters

- Button copy is a micro-message that either confirms or undermines the page's promise. If the headline says 'Book your demo in 2 minutes' but the button says 'Submit,' the visitor wonders: submit what? The mismatch creates cognitive friction. A button that says 'Book free demo' turns the promise into action and lines expectation up with reality.
- Action-oriented copy — naming the benefit or expected outcome — restates the value proposition right at the point of decision. The AIDA model (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action) hinges on clarity: when the button names the gain ('Start analysis,' 'Get my report,' 'Download the guide'), the visitor recognizes their next move without hesitating.
- Action-oriented copy also avoids generic words that read as noise (Next, Submit, Continue). The edge comes from naming the expected outcome — not just the interface.

## How to check your page

- [ ] Does the button name a benefit or outcome rather than a generic word (Submit, Continue, OK)?
- [ ] Does the button copy match the promise made in the headline — without contradicting it or leaving it open-ended?
- [ ] Does the button use first person or an imperative (Get, Start, Book) instead of a vague passive?
- [ ] Do all the primary CTAs on the page — above and below the fold — repeat the same copy or an equivalent message?
- [ ] Is the copy short (2–4 words) for readability, not a paragraph?

## Common mistakes

- **Generic button** — The page says 'Transform your results in 30 days' but the CTA says 'Submit form.' The visitor never connects benefit to action.
- **Headline-button mismatch** — The headline promises a 'personalized demo' but the button just says 'Next.' Friction at the moment of confirming the offer.
- **Passive or hedging copy** — The button says 'Confirm interest' instead of 'Get my proposal.' It drains the sense of action and ownership.
- **Inconsistent labels** — Multiple CTAs across the page, each with different copy (Sign up, Submit, Continue). The single promise gets diluted.

# Tab title that matches the promise

> **Category:** Clarity · **maximum impact**
> Tab titles disconnected from the headline create confusion. How to check the alignment between the <title> meta tag and the visible content.
>
> 🇧🇷 [Versão em português](../../principios/clareza/match-titulo-pagina.md)

When a visitor lands on your page, they see the browser tab (the <title>) before they see the page itself. If the tab says 'Fast, intuitive task-management software' but the visible headline says 'Boost productivity with AI,' there's dissonance. The visitor wonders: 'Wait, is this about AI or about tasks?' That cognitive friction costs clicks. The fix is simple: the promise the <title> starts must carry through to the visible headline. It doesn't have to be identical — it has to be coherent.

## Why it matters

- The <title> is the first point of contact with the visitor. If they click a search result or ad based on the title's promise and the page says something different, they feel the mismatch. Even subconsciously, the effect is churn. Continuity cuts that initial friction.
- UX research shows that coherence between the arrival context (where they came from) and the visual context (what they see) affects how long people stay. A tab that promises X and a page that shows Y is an expectation gap. It doesn't have to be word for word; it has to hold together as one story.
- For SEO, a tab title (meta title) and a visible headline that share a theme signal relevance to both search engines and readers. It's a simple alignment that amplifies the page's overall clarity.

## How to check your page

- [ ] Do the tab title (meta title) and the visible headline communicate the same core promise, or do they reflect different aspects of the product?
- [ ] If you read only the tab title, would you enter the page expecting what the headline delivers? Or would you be surprised?
- [ ] Is the tab title descriptive and specific, or is it generic ('Welcome,' 'Home,' 'Homepage')?
- [ ] Is there a tonal gap between the tab title (more formal/technical) and the visible headline (more conversational)? Is the shift intentional or does it signal a mismatch?

## Common mistakes

- **Vague or generic tab title** — <title>Homepage</title> or <title>Welcome</title> communicates nothing. Be specific: 'CRM for marketing agencies — free trial.'
- **Narrative disconnect between tab and page** — The tab says 'Email software for marketing,' the page says 'Sales automation platform.' Which is it? Align the two or make the relationship clear.
- **Title too long or truncated** — Google and browsers cut off titles past about 60 characters. '<title>Our innovative solution that enables intelligent automation of business processes with AI...</title>' gets clipped; the user only sees 'Our innovative solution that...'.
- **The same title on every page** — Every page reads <title>Home | My Company</title>. Each landing page needs a unique title that reflects its own specific theme.

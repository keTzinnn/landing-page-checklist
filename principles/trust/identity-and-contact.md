# A named company with a way to reach it

> **Category:** Trust · **medium impact**
> Who's behind this? With no name, address, or contact, visitors assume a scam. How to show you're a real company.
>
> 🇧🇷 [Versão em português](../../principios/confianca/identidade-e-contato.md)

A completely anonymous landing page — no company name, no contact email, no address, no social link — triggers instant suspicion. Visitors instinctively ask: 'Who's running this, and why are they hiding?' Even a legitimate product loses trust when nobody can tell who's behind it, before they ever weigh the offer itself. This principle doesn't ask you to splash a physical address across the hero; it just asks that there be a clear way to identify and contact the company.

## Why it matters

- The psychology is simple: people trust entities they can identify. The Nielsen Norman Group finds that pages with an 'about' section, a real address, or a contact channel lower the uncertainty about whether you're legitimate. When those are missing, visitors read it as a sign of a flimsy operation or hidden intent.
- Online scams thrive on anonymity. That's why showing real identity — legal company name, registration number, email, phone, address, or LinkedIn — acts as a psychological barrier against the fraud reflex. A visitor who sees a named company feels safe handing over their details; one who can't tell who you are hesitates, even with a brand they half-recognize.
- For SaaS and subscription products, corporate legitimacy is the baseline expectation. A landing page from 'Acme Solutions' with no about section and no contact in the footer reads as suspect on sight. Showing identity is the norm; deviating from it signals something's off.

## How to check your page

- [ ] Does the company's legal name appear somewhere visible (hero, footer, or about section)?
- [ ] Is there at least one direct contact channel — email, chat, phone, or form — reachable from the page?
- [ ] If there's a footer, does it include an address, phone, email, or social links (LinkedIn, main site)?
- [ ] Does the page link out to the company's main site, corporate LinkedIn, or a public registration?
- [ ] If this is SaaS, is there an 'About' section or a direct link explaining the company, even briefly?
- [ ] On a single-offer landing page (webinar, lead magnet), is the sponsoring company clearly named?

## Common mistakes

- **Fully anonymous page** — Just the product name, no company signature anywhere. The visitor has no idea who's collecting their data, and perceived risk spikes. Add a footer with a name, email, and contact link.
- **Vague or missing contact** — A generic 'talk to sales' with no clickable link doesn't invite action. Add a clickable email, a phone number, or an accessible contact form.
- **Company info buried on a separate page** — If your address and details only live on a Legal/Privacy page, visitors won't find them. A dedicated landing page needs the essentials (name, email/phone) in the footer; legal text can stay on its own page.
- **Identity present but invisible** — Corporate details set in tiny, low-contrast type are effectively absent. Make 'Made by [Company]' legible, in readable body size and adequate contrast.

# Social proof next to the decision button

> **Category:** Trust · **medium impact**
> A beautiful testimonial in an isolated section won't convert like one right by the button. How to place social proof.
>
> 🇧🇷 [Versão em português](../../principios/confianca/prova-social-no-ponto-de-decisao.md)

A page can have incredible testimonials, but if they're all stacked in a 'Happy Customers' section up top while the CTA sits alone at the bottom, the proof doesn't work. The critical moment is when the visitor's hand is over the button, ready to convert — that's when a testimonial reinforces confidence. This principle is about placing social proof strategically, right at the decision point.

## Why it matters

- CXL research on SaaS landing pages shows that social proof near the CTA (in the same view) lowers friction at the moment of decision. The visitor sees a positive comment and gains confidence. If the proof is far away, they've already forgotten it by the time they reach the button. Recency matters: the last element seen before converting often decides the action.
- Cialdini's idea of 'activators' shows that social proof placed far away is passive information. Social proof at the decision point is an action trigger: 'others converted, so I can too.' The brain processes it as validation right before acting. Testing placement — testimonials beside the CTA vs. in an isolated section — often reveals a real conversion difference.
- For dedicated landing pages and forms, the practice is: place social proof (a) above the CTA as early risk reduction, and (b) right after the form or CTA as final reinforcement. Don't dump it all in one separate section — split it and reinforce along the flow.

## How to check your page

- [ ] Is there a testimonial or social proof visible in the same view as the primary CTA (no scrolling required)?
- [ ] If there's a dedicated 'Customers' or 'Testimonials' section, is there also proof right next to (beside or just below) the button?
- [ ] Is the proof near the CTA specific and named (not generic, not anonymous)?
- [ ] On a long page with multiple CTAs, is there reinforcement near the secondary buttons too?
- [ ] Is the social proof at the decision point visually distinct from the isolated testimonials section?
- [ ] Is there visual continuity between the CTA and the nearby proof (same line, enclosing box, alignment)?

## Common mistakes

- **Proof up top, CTA naked at the bottom** — Testimonials sit in a 'Customers' section at the top while the primary CTA is way down with no validation nearby. The visitor saw the proof up top, scrolled the whole page, and forgot it. Reposition: place a short testimonial just above or below the CTA.
- **Generic proof beside the CTA** — Social proof near the CTA but vague ('great product') and anonymous. At the decision point, vagueness hurts. Use a specific testimonial: name, result ('saved 5h/week'). Proximity and specificity together.
- **Proof too faint to read** — A testimonial next to the CTA but in tiny type or washed-out colors doesn't reinforce the decision. Make it stand out: 14–16px body, adequate contrast, maybe a box or a quote icon.
- **Long page, proof only at the top** — A long page needs repeated reinforcement. At each new CTA (or every 3–4 sections), repeat a relevant testimonial. It doesn't waste space — it raises conversion.

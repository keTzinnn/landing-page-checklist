# Fast Reading by Blocks and Headings

> **Category:** Visual Hierarchy · **medium-high impact**
> Dense paragraphs drive readers away. Bullets, subheads, and white space let visitors grasp your landing page in seconds.
>
> 🇧🇷 [Versão em português](../../principios/hierarquia-visual/escaneabilidade.md)

Visitors don't read landing pages word by word — they scan. In a first 3-to-5-second sweep, they're after two things: 'Is this for me?' and 'What's the action?' Hit a wall of text and the brain checks out. A visual structure of short headings, bullets, and short paragraphs works like a map: the eye hops from point to point, assembling understanding fast. This isn't aesthetic preference — it's the neuroscience of reading.

## Why it matters

- Nielsen Norman Group has documented that most visitors read non-linearly rather than sequentially. Eye-tracking research (the F-pattern) shows the eye jumping headline to headline, bullet to bullet, hunting for visual structure. Running text with no hierarchy forces sequential reading and ramps up cognitive effort. With structure — bullets, subheads, visual breathing room — visitors capture the gist without bailing.
- Long paragraphs (more than 3-4 lines) hurt retention and raise bounce. Bullets and lists break up the visual monotony and give quick access to key benefits. The rule is pragmatic: bulleted lists convert better because they're processed in parallel — the reader doesn't have to synthesize sentence by sentence.
- Scannability also lowers cognitive load. Visitors save mental energy for the one decision that matters — converting — instead of spending it figuring out what the page even says. Highly scannable pages are often the top performers in A/B tests, not because they're 'pretty,' but because they're efficient.

## How to check your page

- [ ] Does each section have a descriptive subhead (not a vague one)?
- [ ] Is there at least one block of bullets or a list with 3-5 benefit items?
- [ ] Do body paragraphs run no more than 3-4 lines before a break or a new subhead?
- [ ] Is there white space between sections (the whole page isn't one gray wall)?
- [ ] Is the page graspable in a five-second scan (the eye hopping across headings and bullets)?

## Common mistakes

- **A 10+ line paragraph with no subhead or list to break it up** — Forces linear reading; visitors quit before they finish.
- **Everything in running prose, zero bullets or numbers** — Benefits drown in the text; nothing surfaces as important.
- **Vague subheads ('More Information,' 'Details') with no context** — The scan delivers no understanding; visitors can't tell if it's worth reading on.
- **Uniform visual density across the whole page (same line breaks, same list type)** — Nothing stands out; the entire body reads as uniform visual noise.

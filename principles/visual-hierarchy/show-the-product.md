# Showing the Product for Real

> **Category:** Visual Hierarchy · **medium-high impact**
> A real screenshot, demo, or diagram makes the value tangible. Learn what to show and why words without an image don't convince.
>
> 🇧🇷 [Versão em português](../../principios/hierarquia-visual/visual-do-produto.md)

A SaaS described like this — 'an intuitive platform for project management with real-time collaboration' — stays abstract. Now show a screenshot of the app: a clean dashboard, users collaborating on a visible card. Suddenly the value is tangible. For tools, SaaS, and digital products, the visual is proof. It isn't fluffy marketing; it's evidence of the result. Visitors who SEE the product convert better because they erase the uncertainty — 'this is real, and this is how it works.'

## Why it matters

- Dual Coding Theory (Allan Paivio) shows that combining the verbal (text) and the visual (image) produces better comprehension and retention than either mode alone. In complex digital products especially, the visual resolves ambiguity that text alone can't clear up.
- A screenshot or demo lowers perceived risk. Visitors see the expected output — a real interface, real data, a tangible result. Without a visual, the copy sets an abstract expectation that the actual product may not meet. This matters most on a first purchase, where trust is the dominant factor.
- A product visual also reinforces the message. Pairing image with text lets visitors process the information through more than one channel — a good screenshot is memorable and shareable, while a verbal description alone fades fast. For conversion, retention means return visits and referrals.

## How to check your page

- [ ] Does the page show at least one real screenshot or preview of the product/interface?
- [ ] If it's SaaS, is there an image of the app in use (dashboard, result, or a visible flow)?
- [ ] For non-digital products, is there a clear photo of the result (before/after, in use)?
- [ ] Is the product image above or beside the description, not buried below a lot of copy?
- [ ] If there's a video/demo, is it at least 30 seconds and does it show a real flow (not just a logo or intro)?

## Common mistakes

- **A detailed SaaS description with no screenshot or visible interface** — Visitors can't picture how it works; the value stays vague.
- **A generic image (happy person at a computer) instead of the real product** — Stock photos don't show what the product actually does; expectation drifts from reality.
- **A screenshot so small the interface is illegible** — Visitors can't read the detail and lose the point of the image.
- **A 2-minute demo video with rushed narration or disjointed content** — Visitors run out of patience; a focused 30-60s clip converts better.

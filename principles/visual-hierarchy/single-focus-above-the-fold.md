# A Single Focus at the Top of the Page

> **Category:** Visual Hierarchy · **medium-high impact**
> Two equally weighted buttons split the visitor's attention. Learn why focusing on one primary CTA lifts conversion.
>
> 🇧🇷 [Versão em português](../../principios/hierarquia-visual/foco-unico-acima-da-dobra.md)

Above the fold — the first 800-1000 pixels a visitor sees — there's only mental room for one immediate decision. When the page offers two or more rival CTAs (say, 'Start now' and 'Book a demo' with equal weight), you trigger what psychologists call the paradox of choice: the visitor hesitates, compares, and often leaves without acting. Data-driven practice shows that focusing on one clear primary action above the fold raises conversion because it cuts cognitive friction.

## Why it matters

- Too much choice paralyzes. Barry Schwartz (The Paradox of Choice) and behavioral-economics research document that when a decision-maker faces multiple equally weighted options, anxiety about regret rises — leading to delay or exit. A page that puts 'Subscribe' and 'Request a demo' on same-size buttons forces the visitor to weigh benefits in real time, breaking the flow.
- In conversion marketing, Luke Wroblewski (Web Form Design) establishes that every element above the fold competes for attention in milliseconds. Competing CTAs split that visual budget. The result is fewer clicks on the primary CTA — not because visitors reject it, but because they aren't sure which one is primary.
- The fix isn't to remove alternative paths — it's to organize them into a clear visual hierarchy. The primary CTA (larger, higher contrast, better placed) sits on top. Secondary paths (demo, chat, FAQ) appear below or in a subordinate style. That keeps the focus without taking options away.

## How to check your page

- [ ] Is there just one button/CTA at primary size and contrast above the fold?
- [ ] If there are multiple CTAs, is the secondary one clearly smaller or less saturated?
- [ ] Is the primary CTA placed as the visual point of convergence (center, end of section, or right after the headline)?
- [ ] Are there no two call-to-action links in similar colors within the same block?
- [ ] If there's a chat/messenger widget, is it kept away from the primary CTA (not sitting right beside it)?

## Common mistakes

- **Two side-by-side buttons of equal size and color** — Visitors hesitate over which action to take; one of the two always loses clicks.
- **Primary CTA the same size as 'Terms of Service' or 'Privacy Policy'** — Compliance links get mistaken clicks; the real CTA goes invisible.
- **Multiple CTAs in equally vibrant brand colors** — None stands out; the page seems to ask for several things at once.
- **Primary CTA given the same visual weight as 'Back' or 'Keep browsing'** — Raises bounce by making the exit as prominent as the action you want.
